Color usage rules

  • One gradient per surface. Don't stack brand gradient text on a gradient background.
  • Keep accents in gradient order when numbering — 1 = indigo, 2 = violet, 3 = orange. People read order into it.
  • Never invent mid-stop colors. If you need a solid accent, pick one of the four named stops.

Typography rules

  • Use Inter Tight for anything ≥ 20px. Its native metrics replace the old "set Inter at -0.02em" recipe — don't double up on tight spacing.
  • Inter for body, labels, UI, and captions. Switching faces under 16px keeps counters open and copy readable at small sizes.
  • Uppercase labels always have 0.18em–0.28em tracking. Never uppercase body copy.
  • Gradient text is reserved for nouns that matter — "Google", "Availability", "Floor Plan". Never on full headlines, never on body copy.
  • Print minimum: 12pt body. Labels can go to 10pt if uppercase + tracked.
04 — Layout

Sheet, grid, rhythm.

The canonical surface is a US Letter portrait sheet. Asymmetric L/R padding leaves room for the vertical brand strip. Whitespace and hairlines are the primary dividers — never dotted, never dashed.

Canonical sheet

US Letter portrait at 96dpi with asymmetric internal padding and a 6px brand strip at the top. Content never spills — overflow: hidden.

Icon stroke tokens.

A single 2.5px stroke reads chunky at 16px and thin at 64px. Treat stroke like type size — pick the token that matches the rendered icon size, so icons stay optically consistent from favicon to hero tile.

Implementation note. Heroicons' 24×24 viewBox is authored at stroke-width 1.5. When rendering above the md token, scale the box and override stroke-width to the token value so the stroke stays on-scale optically, not proportional to the source.
06 — Content & copy

Short sentences. Outcomes first.

Headlines are promises. Use the active voice. One idea per sentence — if a comma is doing the work of a period, break it.

On-brand

"3 New Ways to Get Seen on Google."

Outcome first, number as proof. Active verb. No hedging, no adverbs.

Off-brand

"Introducing our innovative new suite of AI-powered tools that can help property management teams potentially improve their Google visibility."

Feature-first, passive, hedged. Too many ideas in one sentence. No reader can repeat it back.

On-brand

"MavenAI turns every community's Google Business Profile into a live marketing surface."

Active voice. Subject does the work. The noun — Google Business Profile — is doing real information lift, not decoration.

Off-brand

"Leverage our comprehensive platform to drive meaningful engagement across your digital footprint 🚀."

Verbs like leverage and drive engagement describe nothing. Emoji replaces a real icon. Reads like a banner ad.

Feature copy pattern

  • Short title (3–5 words) — used in lists and chips. "Services Feed Management."
  • Long title (one line) — used as the H2 on a dedicated feature page. "GBP Services: Amenities + Daily Pricing & Availability."
  • Blurb (one sentence, ≤ 180 chars) — what it does + the user outcome.
07 — Imagery

Real screenshots. Bright architecture.

Product shots are the star. Photography is clean, high-contrast, architecturally legible. Icons stay as line work — never emoji as a substitute.

Imagery rules

  • Product shots — real Google Business Profile UI, MavenAI post cards, floor plan renderings — shown in full color, edge to edge, inside a soft-shadowed white card.
  • Never hand-draw UI screenshots in SVG. If the asset isn't ready, use a subtly-striped placeholder with a monospace label, and ask for the real screenshot.
  • Photography — apartment exteriors, interiors, floor plans — bright, high-contrast, architecturally legible. No moody crops, no people-in-frame hero shots.
  • Icons come from Heroicons (outline set) with rounded terminals. Stroke width follows the icon-stroke token scale — don't use one stroke at every size.

09 — Printing & export

Bleed-less print, crisp exports.

The sheet supplies its own padding. Export presets below keep gradients true and text sharp.

Print

8.5 × 11 portrait, zero margin. The sheet's own padding handles safe area.

Enable -webkit-print-color-adjust: exact so gradients and tinted pills don't drop out.

PPTX export

Export each page at 1632 × 2112 (2× letter) for a crisp screenshot slide.

Keep one slide per page — don't collage two sheets side by side.

10 — Don'ts

Easy ways to break the system.

Mostly about restraint. The gradient is loud on purpose — let it stay a punctuation mark, not a texture.

MavenAI turns every community's Google Business Profile into a live, high-intent marketing surface.
Gradient text on body copy
The gradient is for nouns that matter. On paragraphs, it fights the reader.
Availability
Two saturated stops stacked
Magenta on orange (or any two gradient stops together) collapses contrast. Pick one.
🚀 📈 ✨
Emoji as iconography
Always use the line-icon set. Emoji reads like a deck someone else made.
Boxing every section in a card
Whitespace + hairlines are the primary dividers. Cards should feel earned.
Off-canon gradient angles
92° is the default, 135° is allowed for backdrop washes only. No 45°, no vertical.
MavenAI
Swapping Inter for a "friendly" face
The system reads tech-confident, not playful. Stay in Inter.
